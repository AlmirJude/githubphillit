Desap

by Allan Popa

Analysis

The poem "Desap" by Allan Popa explores themes of waiting, loss, and the trauma of disappearance, often in the context of political oppression. The title "Desap" refers to the Spanish word "desaparecido", meaning "disappeared", which is used to describe individuals who are forcibly abducted, often by government authorities, and whose fate is unknown. This poem poignantly speaks about the pain of waiting for someone who has disappeared, unable to know whether they are alive or dead. It reflects the emotional journey of those left behind in the absence of loved ones.

Elements

  • Perseverance through Challenges:

    Even when faced with obstacles (like the broken branch), one must continue with determination and resilience. The journey of life and love requires persistence and a positive attitude, despite setbacks.

  • Adapting to Fate:

    The line "Kapos kapalaran" teaches that life is unpredictable and sometimes unfair. However, the key is to adjust and keep going forward.

  • Togetherness and Support:

    The speaker encourages Neneng to join him in facing life’s difficulties, showing the importance of having someone to rely on and walk through challenges with.

Themes

The main theme of "Desap" is the enduring pain of waiting for someone who has disappeared. It highlights the trauma of forced disappearances, which often happen in times of political turmoil, where loved ones are left in limbo, neither knowing if their relatives are dead or alive. This theme is directly tied to the historical and political context of the martial law era in the Philippines, when many individuals were abducted and their fates remained unknown.
Additionally, the poem explores memory, time, and loss. Despite the passage of time, the emotional scars of waiting persist, and the longing for resolution never fades.

Reader's POV

From a reader’s perspective, "Desap" can evoke a profound sense of empathy and sorrow for the victims of enforced disappearances and their families. It brings to light the human side of political trauma, focusing not on the event itself, but on the emotional toll of the unknown. The poem may cause the reader to reflect on how political violence affects individuals on a deeply personal level and may resonate with anyone who has experienced waiting or loss without closure.
The imagery in the poem, such as the endless light and the unfaded portrait of waiting, might also evoke feelings of helplessness, as the speaker’s wait seems eternal and unyielding. The reader might feel the tension between hope and despair as the poem beautifully captures the emotional weight of waiting for someone who may never return.

Lessons

The lesson in "Desap" is about the resilience of the human spirit in the face of unimaginable loss. Even though the disappeared may never return, the act of waiting symbolizes the power of hope and love that persists through time, even against the reality of death and uncertainty. The poem also teaches about the permanence of loss and how it can alter the course of one's life, leaving a person forever in a state of longing.
It also serves as a reminder of the consequences of political violence, showing the emotional devastation that occurs when people are taken without explanation. The theme of waiting that does not end with death also suggests the unhealed wounds of injustice, which continue to affect families and societies long after the actual events have passed.
In conclusion, "Desap" is a poignant meditation on the pain of waiting for someone who has disappeared, the toll that time takes on those left behind, and the enduring hope that one day the person might return, even if the end of the waiting is never truly reached.
