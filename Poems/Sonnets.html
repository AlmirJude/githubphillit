Santang Buds

by Alfonso P. Santos

Let me see in dreams the santang buds
That in my absence blossom still beside
My window, Crimson buds, like crimson pearls
Ever faithfulness they bloom, unchanged,
Unfailing like the memories of home.

Now is the time, the season of their blooming,
An hour less, an hour more, yet stays
Their crimson evermore unchanged, untouched.
Let me but see in dreams the santang buds
That in my absence blooms, in faith for one.

Heart lost in foreign lands, fated to share
No love, no fortune from the world but born
To suffer want and misery, decreed
To live unknown, in penitence and need.

Analysis

"Santang Buds" by Alfonso P. Santos is a lyrical poem that expresses deep longing, homesickness, and emotional resilience. The poem uses the image of the santang buds to represent a constant and unchanging connection to the speaker's home, even in the face of physical separation. Through vivid imagery, the poem explores themes of memory, love, sacrifice, and the emotional pain of being far from home.

Elements

  • Santang Buds:

    • The "santang buds" are a central symbol in the poem. The image of the buds blooming in the speaker’s absence reflects the idea of something beautiful and enduring, despite distance or time. The "crimson buds" could symbolize love, passion, and deep emotional ties. The color crimson suggests something precious and full of life, while "like crimson pearls" intensifies the significance of these buds, symbolizing the beauty and value of the speaker’s memories of home.

  • Unchanging Nature of the Buds:

    • The buds are described as "unchanged, unfailing," representing the stability of the speaker’s love and memories. Regardless of time, represented by the "an hour less, an hour more," the buds remain "unchanged"—an image that contrasts with the speaker’s own emotional fluctuations due to his separation from home. The buds’ persistence serves as a symbol of hope and steadfastness.

  • Absence and Longing:

    • The speaker expresses a deep desire to see the buds in dreams—a longing for connection to something that remains unchanged, even though the speaker is far away from it. The act of seeing the buds in dreams suggests that the speaker is physically distant, and the only way to experience that connection to home is through his dreams and memories.

  • Foreign Land and Suffering:

    • The speaker speaks of being "lost in foreign lands" and fated to suffer—this suggests that he is separated from his homeland, and the life he lives now is one of misery and struggle. He is fated to live in a foreign place, far from the love and fortune that he might have had in his home, where he feels both unknown and forgotten. This evokes the pain of displacement and the emotional burden of living without the comforts of home.

Themes

  • Longing and Homesickness:

    • The speaker's deep desire to reconnect with his home, symbolized by the blooming santang buds, reflects the universal theme of homesickness and the emotional challenges of being far from loved ones.

  • Memory and Unchanging Love:

    • The idea that the santang buds remain "unchanged" reflects the enduring nature of memory and love, even when separated by time and space. The unchanging buds symbolize the constancy of the speaker’s love and attachment to his home, which stays alive even in his absence.

  • Sacrifice and Suffering:

    • The speaker speaks of being in a foreign land, suffering without fortune or love, which conveys themes of sacrifice and the pain of being far from home. The poem also touches on the idea of being in a place where one's presence is unknown and unrecognized, highlighting feelings of isolation and exile.

Reader's POV

From a reader’s perspective, "Santang Buds" may evoke strong emotions of nostalgia and yearning. The imagery of the unchanging buds in contrast with the speaker’s own feelings of being lost in a foreign land might resonate with anyone who has experienced separation from their homeland, family, or loved ones. The poem can be viewed as a meditation on the pain of distance and the power of memories to provide solace during difficult times.
The repetition of the unchanging nature of the santang buds may evoke a sense of comfort and hope in the reader, suggesting that even in the hardest of times, memories and emotional connections can remain steadfast. The speaker’s desire to see the buds in dreams also indicates that even though he is physically distant, there is an emotional connection that persists beyond space and time.

Lessons

  • The Endurance of Love and Memory:

    • The unchanging nature of the santang buds symbolizes how love and memories can endure despite physical distance. Even when far away, the emotional bonds we have to people and places can remain constant and comforting.

  • The Pain of Separation:

    • The speaker’s experience of being "lost in foreign lands" and "fated to suffer" teaches about the emotional cost of separation and exile. It speaks to the human experience of displacement, whether due to circumstances, war, or migration, and the struggles that come with it.

  • Hope Amidst Suffering:

    • Despite the speaker’s suffering in a foreign land, his longing to see the santang buds bloom suggests that there is hope even in the darkest moments. The enduring memory of home and love provides strength to persevere, even in the face of hardship.

Conclusion

In conclusion, "Santang Buds" is a powerful reflection on the themes of longing, love, and sacrifice, offering both a personal and universal message about the resilience of memory and the emotional power of connections to home. The poem speaks to the strength that can be drawn from memories of loved ones and the constancy of love, even when physical presence is no longer possible.
